About Me

I'm a GIS Specialist with graduate training in Geographic Information Science & Technology from UMass Amherst and over two years of professional experience. My work has supported nonprofit initiatives, transportation planning, and environmental stewardship, where I've applied GIS to make data more accessible and actionable. I've developed dashboards used by social workers and legal professionals, digitized transportation records to improve planning workflows, and introduced GIS tools into volunteer programs to support ecological restoration. Along the way, I've focused on creating GIS solutions that are accurate, accessible, and easy to use, while adapting to new tools and workflows quickly.

Explore the map to see how I've contributed to projects across organizations!

Outside of formal projects, I enjoy exploring how GIS can enrich my own everyday experiences. Living in Boston, I've put together scratchwork web maps of places around the city, collected GPS data while foraging plants, and experimented with open-source tools like OsmAnd & Leaflet to see how maps can work offline and at low-cost. These side projects keep me connected to the practical and creative sides of mapping, fueling my curiosity and giving me new ways to engage with the places I care about. They remind me that GIS isn't only a professional skill — it's also a personal passion that shapes how I see and experience the world around me.
